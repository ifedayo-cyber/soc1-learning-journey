A S**OC Level 1 analyst** works 24/7 in a SOC team and mostly reviews the security alerts together with their colleagues. To do this efficiently, you will need practice and skills. A day in the life will consist of:
. Monitoring and Investigating various security alerts
. Participating in SOC brainstorms and workshops
. Cooperating with other teams to keep your company safe
. Constantly learning and discovering new attacks and defenses 

The truth about being a Security Analyst:
Being in the defensive frontline is not easy, as you have to constantly learn new things. During a busy 8-hour shift, you might be buried under a mountain of "tickets" - the alerts and tasks that you need to resolve in a timely manner. Still, the job is fun and rewarding, especially after you stop a real threat from damaging your organization. Even better, it is fascinating to know how the attacks you hear about in the news actually happen in the real world.

I learnt about some open-source databases out there like AbuseIPDB, and Cisco Talos Intelligence, where you can perform a reputation and location check for the IP address. Most security analysts use these tools to aid them with alerts investigations. You can make the internet safer by reporting the malicious IPs on AbuseIPDB. 

\* I used the TryHackMe tool to Identify malicious IP address via SIEM, I used the IP hunter to perform a reputation and location check for the IP address, I escalated the event by declaring a small incident event and escalating it to my SOC Team Lead. While my team lead is analyzing the alert in more detail, I got the permission to block the malicious IP address on the firewall to slow down the attack.
