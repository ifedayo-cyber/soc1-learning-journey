**SOC ROL**E in Blue Team

This space teaches you about where SOC L1 analyst role is placed in a company structure, who oversees the team, what other security department exists, and which skills you need to advance through your career ladder. 

High-level example:

**Executives**: (CEO, CFO, Company owner): Focuses on global business objectives

**Security Leadership**: CTO, CIO, CISO (Chief Information Security Officer): Leads company-wide IT or security programs

**Security Managers:** Red Team Lead/SOC Manager: They manage a single team or department like SOC

**Technical:** Analyst/Engineer/Red Teamer: Perform technical tasks like log analysis.

**Security Operations Center (SOC)**: This is where you are most likely to begin your cyber security journey. The SOC is the central hub for an organization's cyber security- also known as the first line of defense. Here you work with various alerts and handle most attacks. 

The SOC is composed of the following roles:
**. L1 Analysts**: Junior members who triage and pass complex cases to L2
**. L2 Analysts:** Experienced members who investigate more advanced attacks
**. Engineers:** Experts in configuring security tools like EDR or SIEM
**. Manager:** A person who manages the whole SOC team

**SOC Team:** Usually Operates 24/7, Creates Detection Rules, Investigates Security Alerts, Collects and Monitors Logs, Cooperates with IT Team, Writes Summary Reports.

**Cyber Incident Response Team (CIRT):** If SOC expertise is not enough or the incident goes out of control, you urgently call the firefighters - CIRT, also called CSIRT, or CERT. The members have a broad knowledge of cyber threats and handle breaches without depending on tools like EDR or SIEM. A CIRT job is stressful and responsible, but also rewarding.
**Team members:** CIRT Manager, Forensics Lead, Threat Intel Expert, Threat Hunting Expert, Malware Analyst.

**CIRT:** Usually Called On-Demand, Supports SOC with Analysis, Handles Critical Incidents, Identifies Hidden Threats, Performs Deep Forensics, Recovers Breached Systems.

**Specialized Defensive Roles:** 
**. Digital Forensics Analyst:** Uncovers hidden threats in disk and memory
**. Threat Intelligence Analyst:** Gathers data about emerging threat groups
**. AppSec Engineer:** Maintains a secure software development lifecycle
**. AI Researcher:** Studies AI threats and how to defend against them
**. DevSecOps**
. **Penetration Tester
. GRC Auditor** 

Internal SOC vs MSSP:
Not every organization has the expertise to operate a SOC on its own and relies on a Managed Security Services Provider (MSSP), a company that delivers outsourced security services, most commonly SOC, to its clients. Working at MSSP is typically high-pressure, but it is also a good option to quickstart your career. Although I recommend applying for any open SOC position as your first job.

Next Steps



Your most natural next step after L1 is to become a SOC L2 analyst, but you are free to choose another path! While handling a SIEM alert, you might notice that engineering work appeals to you more. During a cyber attack, you may be fascinated by CIRT actions. You may also find yourself well-suited as a manager and build your path to the CISO role. No matter what, your first year or two is to get real work experience, and to spend this time effectively, follow the tips below.

TIPS:
**. Learn From Every Alert:** Understand why a rule triggered and use it to sharpen your detection skills
**. Think Like An Attacker:** Ask "Why would the attackers do it" before triaging how did they do it
**. Verify Everything:** Never assume. Always validate alerts and suspicious behavior in logs
**. Get Involved in Incidents:** Real attacks teach lessons no lab can. They are worth a sleepless night
