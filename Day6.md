**SOC L1 Alert Triage**



Today, I learned about the core concept for any SOC team - "**Alert**". Knowing how to handle it determines whether a security breach is detected and prevented, or missed and devastating. Alerts and notifications generated by a security solution when a specific event or sequence of events occurs save SOC analysts from manual log review by highlighting only suspicious, anomalous events. With alerts, analysts triage just dozens of alerts per day instead of millions of raw logs.



Alert Management Platforms:

|Solution|Examples|Description|
|-|-|-|
|SIEM System|Splunk ES, Elastic|SIEMs have solid alert management capabilities and are a perfect choice for most SOC teams|
|EDR or NDR|MS Defender, CrowdStrike|While EDR and NDR provide their own alert dashboards, it is preferred to use SIEM or SOAR|
|SOAR System|Splunk SOAR, Cortex SOAR|Bigger SOC teams can use SOAR to aggregate and centralize alerts from multiple solutions|
|ITSM System|Jira, TheHive|Some teams may have a custom ticket management (ITSM) setup using a dedicated solution<br /><br />|





Everyone in the SOC team is somehow involved in the alert triage:



&#x20;  SOC L1 analysts:  Review the alerts, distinguish bad from good, and notify L2 analysts in case of a real threat

&#x20;  SOC L2 analysts:  Receive the alerts escalated by L1 analysts and perform deeper analysis and remediation

&#x20;  SOC engineers:  Ensure the alerts contain enough information required for efficient alert triage

&#x20;  SOC manager:  Track speed and quality of alert triage to ensure that real attacks won't be missed
