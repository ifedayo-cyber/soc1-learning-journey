**SOC:** Also known as Security Operations Center is a dedicated facility operated by a specialized security team. This team aims to continuously monitor an organization's network and resources and identify suspicious activity to prevent damage. It's goal is to **detect and respond**. There are 3 pillars of the SOC and they are:
1. **People:** They help the security solution to identify truly harmful activities and enables prompt response.
2. **Process:** \* Alert triage is the basis of the SOC team. The first response to any alert is to perform a triage. It is all about answering the 5Ws (Who, What, Where, When, Why).
3. **Technology**: It refers to the security solutions. They efficiently minimize the SOC's teams manual effort to detect and respond to threats. Some of the security solutions are SIEM, EDR, Firewall.

**SIEM:** Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) is a popular tool that collects logs from various network devices, referred to as log sources. Detection rules are configured in the SIEM solutions which contains logic to identify suspicious activity.

**EDR**: Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR): provides the SOC team with detailed real-time and historical visibility of the device's activities. It operates on the endpoint level and can carry out automated responses. 

**Firewall:** It functions for network security and acts as a barrier between your internal and external networks such as the internet.
